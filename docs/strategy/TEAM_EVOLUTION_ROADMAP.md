
# Team Evolution Roadmap (2026-2029)

**Status:** DRAFT - Awaiting Founder Review  
**Author:** Chief of Staff (webwakaagent2)  
**Date:** February 3, 2026

---

## 1. Mandate

This document outlines the planned evolution of the WebWaka Agentic Software Factory team structure to support the platform's 10-year vision. It is a living document that will be updated annually.

## 2. Phased Team Expansion

### **Year 1 (2026): Foundational Team (14 Agents)**

- **Focus:** Establish core engineering, product, and governance capabilities.
- **Structure:** The 14-agent structure as defined in the **Final Team Structure Proposal**.

### **Year 2 (2027): Platform Primitives Team (17 Agents)**

- **Focus:** Build out the foundational primitives for the platform-for-platforms vision.
- **New Roles (3):**
    - **Real-Time Systems Engineer:** Owns the WebSocket and real-time infrastructure.
    - **Module SDK Engineer:** Develops and maintains the partner-facing Module SDK.
    - **Marketplace Engineer:** Builds the generic marketplace primitives.

### **Year 3 (2028): Platform Expansion Team (20 Agents)**

- **Focus:** Expand the platform's capabilities into new domains.
- **New Roles (3):**
    - **Social Graph Engineer:** Owns the social graph and content management primitives.
    - **Geolocation Engineer:** Owns the geolocation service layer and data models.
    - **Compliance Engineer:** Develops and maintains the pluggable compliance modules.

### **Year 4+ (2029 and beyond): Scale & Specialization (22-25 Agents)**

- **Focus:** Scale the platform and deepen specialization.
- **Potential New Roles (2-5):**
    - **Mobile Lead:** Dedicated owner of the mobile experience.
    - **Integration Engineer:** Focuses on third-party integrations.
    - **Developer Relations Advocate:** Engages with the partner ecosystem.
    - **AI/ML Engineer:** Specializes in the AI Orchestration Layer.
    - **Data Scientist:** Analyzes platform usage and provides insights.

## 3. Rationale

This phased approach ensures that the team structure evolves in lockstep with the platform's architectural and strategic priorities. It avoids over-staffing ahead of readiness while ensuring that the necessary expertise is in place to support each new phase of development.
